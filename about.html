Blackfoot Language: Rematriation Of Culture And Identity

Language, in most ‘Western-speaking societies, believe it serves a singular purpose: communication. In Indigenous societies, especially those societies that are ‘oral’, language is much more than just about communication. Language becomes the repository for thousands upon thousands of years of knowledge. That knowledge is expressed through storytelling. Those stories, it can be said, are documentations of history, science, culture, customs, and traditions. Language is a source of cultural identity and carries with it the social values of a society. In other words, language has specific contents and expressions that are unique to a culture that bring about a sense of identity and belonging. Language is the ‘short cut’ to understanding the worldview of a society. Blackfoot, as an oral language, is an example of all those functions and purposes that a language serves in addition to communication. In science, education, politics, economics, and social organizations Blackfoot brings about views and ways of knowing ‘Western-based languages cannot express or bring about as knowledge…..hence, the axiology of Blackfoot as a source of knowledge. Being ‘Blackfoot’ means ‘living the language’.
